Rewrote `WorkerPlugins::AddQuery` to rely on the existing `unique_resource_on_workspace` composite index to dedupe at INSERT time instead of running a `WHERE NOT EXISTS` anti-join. The previous form ran the same anti-join twice — once via `pluck(primary_key)` to populate `created` and again inside `INSERT ... SELECT` — roughly doubling the wall time on large candidate sets (60s+ on ~340k users). The new form does one cheap index-only pluck of candidate primary keys, one cheap index-only scan of the workplace's existing links, and a single streaming INSERT that relies on a dialect-specific conflict clause to skip duplicates: `INSERT IGNORE` on MySQL/MariaDB, `INSERT OR IGNORE` on SQLite, and `INSERT ... ON CONFLICT (workplace_id, resource_type, resource_id) DO NOTHING` on PostgreSQL.
